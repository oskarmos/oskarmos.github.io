Cheesy Clich Designer text, a Designer who wants the world to be sustainable, recyclable, and ecological but wants to follow modern design trends, heard that somewhere already, right?
It is not uncommon to hear this nowadays, mostly because we live in a world of consumerism, trash, global warming, and a LOT of greenwashing.
Im a consumer too, we all are, but for me, it bothers me how every time you want to go with a sustainable, out of all sudden you have to pay far more than a baseline product. Oh and not to mention how ugly sustainable options usually are, where did the aesthetics go?


As a tinkerer and DIY-er I stumble upon another problem, repairability.
Companies love to make products that are nearly unrepairable yourself, and many times even if you try to do it yourself, they will lock you out of major features of the device youre working on.
Well, it is rather logical why companies love to do this, profit, am I right?
Well, on behalf of recyclable, sustainable, and ecological design, I am already on my way to researching composites and how we can make non-polymer-based resins, and how to utilize fibers, first researching how other composites work and their properties.
Id also like to dip my toes into business, to try to see how I could contribute to fixing the problem of sustainable products being pricey.
And repairability? Make products in the future that can be repaired, so that way I can contribute a tiny bit towards a better world.
