First of all, I used to be a freelance graphic designer, going under the same name as this website, but eventually decided that it isn’t what I want to pursue my career into as I started getting deeper and deeper into it. It all started when I was approximately 11 years old and started a successful YouTube channel, and I didn’t want to pay for the designs, so I started learning Photoshop and Illustrator myself. After a year or two I started doing designs for local bars and restaurants (designs of the menus and brand identity), simply kept going with that, to eventually making posters for my school and my local town hall, after that I went into a more worldwide approach, making posters and designs for customers from all around the world.

That being said, I have a lot of experience in Photoshop and Illustrator, which are useful for the study Industrial Deisgn.

Next thing to mention is Advanced Technical Drawing, in high school I chose Technical Drawing as an elective, and my teacher considered that we were more capable than what the educational system would make us learn, so she taught us how to do technical drawings with all the ISO Standards, engineering methods and notations, and extreme precision, having up to 10 assignments per week, where every assignment would take an hour to do.
At the time I hated it, it was extremely difficult, as we would get punished for any not precise drawings, but reflecting back, I learned a lot.

By the end of the course, at the exams issued by the government, we all got a mark of 10/10, making us the highest-marked school in the entirety of Spain.
Another relevant thing I learned in the past is 3D Modeling.
Initially I started by trying to model a “dream room” before a house makeover, making it all in SketchUp, eventually learning how to use Fusion 360 as SketchUp started to limit my abilities.
Eventually I started to make 3D Printed models for my driving simulator that I had at home, and there’s not much to it.
As I mention 3D Models for my driving simulator, I must mention that I did the UX/UI in the Alpha version of the program SimHub, SimHub is a program where you can add external hardware to your simulator, also you can connect it to your phone and have a virtual gauge cluster. Also made a few of the free downloadable gauge clusters.
This gave me experience with UX/UI, coding in C#, and also experience in playing around with Arduino and ESP32.

Next on the list is woodworking. I have an unfinished woodworking license/degree, as I took the course when I was living in Canada. Since I moved to another country, I couldn’t really continue or finish what I was doing. But all the skills, and craftsmanship knowledge, alongside all the knowledge how to operate all the tools is still there, and I still try to use them nowadays, but with how expensive lumber is these days, it’s hard.

To briefly mention, I used to sketch and draw a lot, not the greatest at it, but it is a skill to showcase >>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>

I also have a large background in video editing, but I did this more for fun. I have skills therefore in numerous video editing softwares, which include: Sony Vegas Pro (in which I started), Adobe Premiere Pro and DaVinci Resolve Studio.
Summer 2020 I did a course on video color correction, after which I did a mini internship of one project with the Blackmagic Design Team, where I learned a lot about working ethics and similar, gaining a lot from being around that professional field for a few days.

I also did take a course in school in Canada on Technical Design, that taught me a bit of the Design process we use nowadays.

And last but not least, 2020-2021 I took numerous online courses on AutoDesk programs, but I haven’t really learned much from it as it was too much information at once. I do know how to navigate the programs around a bit, but not enough to call myself fluent.
