Ive developed quite largely this year, not only as a designer in the expertise areas of ID, but also as a person.
Looking back at my PDP goals, I have achieved or tackled most of my goals, alongside goals taking different directions and new ones forming on the way.
In my first PDPs I mentioned I wanted to tackle ergonomy in some way - I did.
Improve my sketching - I did. Make sustainable and visually appealing designs - I did. Improve my 3D Modeling - I did. Develop my own style - done. Learning to present my ideas - done. Do research about composites and do my own development in my own house - done.
Learned a lot of things that I didnt plan, but that has such an influence on me right now, such as dealing with the user, making designs centered around the user, brainstorming, and quick-and-dirty prototyping.

My growth clearly has been the biggest in Technology and Realization, Creativity and Aesthetics, and User and Society, and although less than the two aforementioned, I also learned quite a bit about Business and Entrepreneurship.
Meanwhile, Math, Data, and Computing were more left over on the side, as it was simply academic knowledge I knew before High School and needed to refresh. I plan to tackle those in the next year.
I started the study with an open mindset to learning about creativity and aesthetics, arguably one of the most pleasurable expertise areas you can learn in my opinion, beginning with a very messy idea in my head about brainstorming and artistic experience from before.
I consider that the From Idea to Design course really organized my mind, with multiple ideation methods such as brainwriting and mind mapping. Meanwhile the course Exploratory Sketching really brought visualization of an idea straight to the paper. This has helped me to generate, change and refine ideas - essentially brainstorm.
I believe these methods will stay with me forever as a Designer, and I assume Ill get better and faster with each attempt to brainstorm.

On behalf of aesthetics, I started developing my own recognizable style, and finally being satisfied with the results of my work, reaching pride in my own designs, something I did not have before.
Learned a lot about aesthetics during the project in From Idea to Design, same as in Project 1, knowledge then fortified and unified by the course Exploratory Sketching.

An odd place to learn creativity and aesthetics was the course Creative Programming. I did not expect to learn about generative design, I assumed the course would be purely programming.
This has also taught me that combining things Ive learned from different expertise areas should be something I should do in the future, to form a communicative, coherent and in the case of the course Creative Electronics, functional.
Technology and Realization is a big part of my design portfolio, I consider it essential to make sustainable, practical and real-world solutions.
Beginning from the course of Creative Mechanical Design, Engineering and Manufacturing, where I expanded my horizons in the knowledge of rapid prototyping, gathering new experience in the world of 3D Printers, laser cutters, laser scanners (and fixing the data gathered from laser scanners), among others.

Whilst those were simply expansions of my previous known knowledge, finite element modeling was the new part, alongside simulations (finite element analysis), which later has proven to be big help in Project 1.
Another new thing I learned was manufacturing methods and manufacturing costs. This has gasped a lot of attention in me, to the point that after finishing the course, I kept researching, and learning more about such, opening my eyes to what I eventually learned about composites.

Composites, this has turned out to be a large part of my development unexpectedly. I did want to learn about this, but I thought I dont have enough knowledge to do so, and Ill tackle this maybe in the third year of Industrial Design. Through the few months, Ive learned tabout different types of composites, tensile strength, types of resin, epoxy bonding, all types of hardeners, and manufacturing methods.
I spoke to experts, during Design Expos such as Dutch Design Week, and went to Deurne to a company that manufactures buses out of composites to talk to experts, and this summer, I plan to do an internship at that company to further develop my knowledge.
This has also brought me to work with the real-world industry, sending CAD files to CNC plotters, learning about different materials for the molds, bonding techniques, it was a very broadening experience.

Whilst I have had a bit of experience before TU/e with electronics, I always disliked it a bit, fortunately, the course Creative Electronics has taken that stigma out of me, teaching me a more analog way of approaching electronics, and a far more fun approach too. I could then apply that knowledge when making my prototype for Project 1, testing my circuits in the E-Lab and prototyping.

I expanded my knowledge through Creative Programming, by programming WS2812B type LEDs, learning about the FastLED library, to then use that knowledge in Creative Electronics to continue learning about Individually Addressable LEDs, as I plan to use these in my future design projects.

Also, in those courses I learned breaking down issues to its bare essentials, and making sure everything works, having this way also a physical version and digital version of debugging as a problem solving activity.
I cant mention User and Society without mentioning the course User-Centered Design.
This course has taught me how to make surveys, interviews, focus groups, personas, etc. Then I could apply this knowledge to Project 1.
I see making designs around the user, core knowledge, allowing us to do iterations and decisions based on the data gathered from the potential user, that way viewing the entire Design from an outside perspective.

Practicing UCD principles taught me how to develop fairer design processes through cross-user engagement, and I see myself using a lot of this knowledge in the future of my projects.
The knowledge gathered from the course Data Analytics for Engineers came hand in hand with User and Society, as we could analyze the data we gathered or were given, in my example, ergonomic data for the chair made for Project 1.

USE Basic course has given me an interesting idea about the psychology of the user, helping in the development of Project 1, by centering our design on building habits, such as the habit of taking breaks from your computer.

This involved a lot of psychological analysis, and experimentation with behavior change technologies, having to keep ethics in mind at all times.
Having to perform moral analysis during our final stages of Project 1, has helped to clear conclusions, and made our decisions more precise.
Having to take Risk Management in mind was also something new. I combined that with the data gathered from finite element analysis, weighing the risks and benefits to see if our product is ethical and viable to use on a daily basis.

Id like to develop further in the future around the user, whilst we conducted user testing, interviews, and such, Id like to continue involving users in my designs, as it clears any assumptions I may have, preventing deceptive decisions.
I learned in this expertise area only from making Projects with my peers, I would consider myself a pretty entrepreneurial person. In my view, B&E involves finding opportunities and possibilities to create something valuable for the stakeholders.

I learned the most by contacting and visiting companies for Project 1, having hand in hand experience with the real world and slowly figuring out how it all works. Conversations with the stakeholders responsible for the companies have brought me an insight that I could tie hand in with the expertise area of User and Society.
Alongside, through first learning manufacturing costs and methods in the course of Creative Mechanical Design, Engineering and Manufacturing, I further had to learn through my partners and research about things such as MSRP, Profit-Margins, and different methods of providing services and selling services, etc.
This wasnt in my plan to learn at all, yet I am very grateful that I have learned this as it has opened my eyes to a lot of things.
A lot of it was also disappointing, a hit of the real-world, as we contacted a hundred companies in the Netherlands, with barely any reception back, as no one is available or reachable.

Through the year I have learned how to better and better present the idea to the stakeholders, by either doing presentations and expositions or showcasing them to potential customers.
This has helped me to grow my confidence, and make my speech much more trustworthy, gaining my own methods as time went by.

In From Idea to Design and Project 1, I learned with my colleagues how to find gaps in the market, by finding opportunities and checking existing design solutions for our problem. By doing this, I understood better how to improve my design, and what could differentiate my design from other existing ones, analyzing the mistakes and errors that the other makers of the products we were analyzing made.

Next academic year Id like to extend this expertise area, as I first wanted some sort of foundation in Design before starting with business and entrepreneurship.
For this expertise area, I expanded my previous knowledge of Mathematics and Physics, I didnt know how was it possible to link this to the Design world, but as it turns out, in Creative Programming, my entire design was very much math and physics, as I used sine and cosine to generate waves and to fluctuate the colors in a non randomized manner. This was my first step to linking Math, Data and Computing to Design. Then all I have learned in Data Analysis for Engineers was applied for understanding the Data gathered for ergonomy in Project 1. I plan to use Data Analysis in the future, as it directly ties to User and Society.
I hope to learn more about artificial intelligence and get better at making algorithms in the future.

I also participated in a Hackathon during the carnival week, in particular one to help Ukraine. Our team made an entire app and algorithm that avoids potential Russian occupation or tanks, to get to the closest rescue point. We got a proud second place.
This has helped me to learn in C# and Python, and make rapid UI Prototypes with HTML and Adobe XD. Also learned how to connect the front end with the back end of the software.

Next year I plan to further develop my knowledge in this expertise area, and I aim to integrate it into my Design Projects.
I can say whilst its still not the greatest, I improved my organization skills and collaboration skills, mainly through working with my peers, and being the head of the team of Project 1. I learned how to better analyze and implement the feedback we receive, and also learned how to better give feedback.

I still need to improve task division, as I usually take most of the work onto my own back, overworking myself from time to time. Talking to companies and going to events has helped me in improving my communication and networking, also with the help of TU/e Career and Skills, where I attended a few workshops to expand my horizons.
I also learned a bit of Dutch, only up to A2 level, but I want to learn more and more, to get better at networking events, as theres many Dutch companies.
